Chapter 9 Subjective Inequality

A less developed aspect is subjective inequality

  • Preferences (A. Alesina & Giuliano, 2010). Este book chapter es una combinación literature review con evidencia de USA.
  • Perception of economic inequality (García-Sánchez et al., 2019).
  • Reference group and Reality (M. D. R. Evans et al., 1992; M. D. R. Evans & Kelley, 2004, 2017)
  • Welfarism, higher class fatigue (M. Evans & Kelley, 2018)

However, this body of literature has paid less attention to the role of perception on the emergence of redistributive preferences, especially how people perceive their standing on the social ladder, conceptualised as subjective social status (SSS) (M. D. R. Evans & Kelley, 2004; Lundberg & Kristenson, 2008; Słomczyński & Kacprowicz, 1986).
