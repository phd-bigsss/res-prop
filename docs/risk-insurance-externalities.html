Externalities. We propose that poor people (regardless of their expected future income) value redistribution for its immediate tax and transfer consequences. The redistributive preferences of the rich, on the other hand, are less significantly affected by current tax and transfer considerations. For the rich, the negative externalities of inequality can become more relevant. (Rueda & Stegmueller, 2019, p. 89)
