Chapter 3 Self-interest, Expected income, Social mobility

  • self-interest hypothesis (Dimick et al., 2018; Meltzer & Richard, 1981),

why the rich would be in favour of higher levels of redistribution? A. Alesina & Giuliano (2010) posit two factors: positive externalities of education, reduce crime and secure property

On the other hand, another factor the incentives generated by inequality as an aggregate social value. Greater inequality means that individuals would have to work harder and become more educated

Mengel & Weidenholzer (2022) says that Wealth versus Income Inequality are different. In this regard, Steele et al. (2022) compared income and wealth as drivers of redistributive preferences.

  • Prospective upward mobility (POUM) (Benabou & Ok, 2001; Fong, 2004; Jaime-Castillo, 2008)
  • Social Mobility (SM) (Alberto Alesina et al., 2018; Jaime-Castillo & Marqués-Perales, 2019; Valdimarsdóttir et al., 2022)

Education can be considered as proxy for mobility prospects. Educational mobility does not have an effect. However, occupational mobility does it. (A. Alesina & Giuliano, 2010)

How previous research intended to capture the influence of prospects about future income relies mainly on subjective expectations about future abstract status position (Alberto Alesina & La Ferrara, 2005). In this regard, Rueda & Stegmueller (2019) discuss this approach because it only considers short periods in time, not the complete life cycle.

future_income = age + age2 + education + age*education + e

They assume that individuals have some knowledge about life-cycle income profile of people with their same level of education (Rueda & Stegmueller, 2019, p. 41)

  • expectations of income are not related to risk aversion

  • an individual demands more redistribution today, for example, if she knows she will be poorer in the future

  • correlation of future income is low (0.2 to 0.3) with actual income.

  • estimaron el ingreso futuro usando ols

Ideas: - cuánto crees que esta persona gana - cuánto crees que esta persona va ganar en X años - cuánto crees que esta persona debería ganar

Rueda & Stegmueller (2019) “the importance of present income as a determinant of redistribution preferences is highly variable, both cross-nationally and temporally.”

lifetime income model based on labour economics that considers education and experience as key drivers of income, therefore, preferences for redistribution. The key concepts here are social insurance, risk and mobility (Rueda & Stegmueller, 2019)

The prospective social mobility model assumes that the tax revenues are fixed for both, origin and destination (A. Alesina & Giuliano, 2010)
